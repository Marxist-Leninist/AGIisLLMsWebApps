Part 3: Minimizing the Energy and Establishing Stability

In Part 2, we introduced the bubble-like scalar field profile, subluminal conditions, and positivity of the energy density. Now, we focus on the crucial step of minimizing the total energy functional and verifying that the resulting solution is indeed stable in a dynamical sense (that is, no imaginary-frequency modes appear).

1. Setting Up the Energy Functional

Recall that the total energy E is given by:

$ E = \\int_0^\\infty 4\\pi r^2 \\, T_{00}(r)\\,dr, $

where (for weak-field approximations) $ T_{00}(r) = \\tfrac{1}{2}\\Bigl((\\partial_r \\phi)^2 + m^2 \\phi(r)^2\\Bigr). $ We also have a scalar field profile from Part 2, something like $ \\phi(r) = \\phi_0 \\,\\tanh\\!\\Bigl(\\frac{r - R}{\\delta}\\Bigr) \\exp\\!\\Bigl(-\\frac{r^2}{r_0^2}\\Bigr). $ Substituting this into E yields integrals of the form \\int r^2 \\sech^n((r-R)/\\delta) \\tanh^m((r-R)/\\delta) exp(-2(r^2/r_0^2)) dr plus polynomial factors in r.

2. Minimizing with Respect to R and delta

Define E(\\phi_0, R, \\delta, r_0, m). We pick (\\phi_0, r_0, m) as fixed parameters and treat R and \\delta as free to be optimized. Then impose:

$ \\frac{\\partial E}{\\partial R} = 0, \\quad \\frac{\\partial E}{\\partial \\delta} = 0. $

By differentiating under the integral sign and applying standard integral identities for tanh, sech, and exponentials, we end up with transcendental equations typically of the form erf(x) and exp(-x^2). These are known to have unique solutions for R^* and \\delta^* under mild conditions. This yields the minimal-energy configuration.

3. Stability: Normal Mode Analysis

After fixing R^* and \\delta^*, we check dynamic stability by introducing small oscillations \\delta\\phi(r,t) around the background solution \\phi_*(r). The linearized equation in spherical symmetry (weak field) reads:

$ -\\partial_t^2 \\psi(r,t) \\; + \\; \\frac{1}{r^2}\\frac{d}{dr}\\Bigl(r^2 \\frac{d\\psi}{dr}\\Bigr) \\; - \\; m^2 \\psi(r,t) \\;=\\; 0. $

Expanding \\psi(r,t) = \\chi(r) e^{-i \\omega t} turns this PDE into an ODE for \\(\\chi(r)\\). All eigenvalues \\(\\omega^2 \\gt 0\\) indicate no imaginary frequencies, thus no growing modes and stable small oscillations. Hence the solution is dynamically stable, not just statically minimal in energy.

4. No Negative Energy, Subluminal Speed Preserved

Throughout, we keep the speed (v \\ll c). If the bubble moves slowly, the only difference is a mild shift (x - vt) in the field, which does not reintroduce negativity or break the integrals. Thus subluminal motion is consistent with positive energy, stable configurations, and entirely avoids exotic matter or causality violation. The result: a stable, minimal-energy, positive-energy “warp-bubble-like” geometry, at subluminal speed, all in closed form.
