Nuclear Isomer Battery: Full Analytical Solution

This document presents a closed-form analytical solution for the time evolution and power output of a theoretical nuclear isomer battery under triggered conditions. The model assumes:

  • We have a large number N0 of identical nuclear isomers initially in an excited state with energy E* above the ground state.
  • The isomer has a half-life T1/2, giving a spontaneous decay constant λ = ln(2)/T1/2.
  • At time t0, we apply a triggering mechanism (e.g., a suitable photon beam) that induces an additional decay width Γtrigger, speeding up the release of stored energy.
  • Γspont = ħ λ = ħ ln(2)/T1/2 and Γtrigger can be expressed in terms of known nuclear structure parameters, the reduced transition probability B(Xℓ), and photon field intensity and energy.

Population Evolution

Before triggering (t < t0), the number of excited nuclei N(t) decays spontaneously:

N(t) = N_0 e^{-λ t}

At t = t0, we turn on the trigger, adding Γtrigger. For t ≥ t0:

N(t) = N_0 e^{-λ t_0} e^{-(λ + Γ_trigger/ħ)(t - t_0)}

Combine the exponentials:

N(t) = N_0 e^{-λ t - (Γ_trigger/ħ)(t - t_0)H(t-t_0)}

Here H(t-t0) is the Heaviside step function, ensuring Γtrigger applies only after t0.

Power Output

The instantaneous power output P(t) is the energy released per unit time. Each decay releases E* energy. The total decay rate after triggering is (λ + Γtrigger/ħ) in frequency units. To keep consistent units, note that Γspont = ħ λ and Γtrigger are widths with energy dimension, so the rate in s-1 is λ for spontaneous and Γtrigger/ħ for trigger-induced:

P(t) = N(t) E^* (\Gamma_{spont} + \Gamma_{trigger})/\hbar

Since Γspont = ħ ln(2)/T1/2, substitute back λ if desired. Before triggering (t < t0):

P(t) = N_0 E^* λ e^{-λ t}

After triggering (t ≥ t0):

P(t) = N_0 E^* \frac{\Gamma_{spont}+ \Gamma_{trigger}}{\hbar} e^{-\Gamma_{spont} t_0/\hbar} e^{-(\Gamma_{spont}+\Gamma_{trigger})(t-t_0)/\hbar}

Where Γspont = ħ λ. This is a fully explicit closed-form solution.

Triggering Width from Nuclear Structure

The triggered decay width Γtrigger can be related to nuclear structure parameters. For an E1-type electromagnetic transition, a simplified formula (example) might be:

Γ_{trigger} \approx \frac{16\pi}{9}\alpha \frac{B(E1)}{\hbar (m_p c^2)} (E_{\gamma})^2 I_{\gamma}

Here:

  • α = fine-structure constant
  • B(E1) = reduced transition probability for the E1 transition
  • m_p = proton mass (or appropriate mass scale)
  • E_γ = photon energy used to trigger the transition
  • I_γ = photon intensity

By substituting this expression for Γtrigger into the power output and population formulas, we get a fully analytic closed-form solution for any given set of nuclear and experimental parameters.

Conclusion

The above equations provide a closed-form analytical solution for the population decay and power output of a nuclear isomer battery both in spontaneous and triggered regimes. These formulas can be directly used to evaluate the performance of the battery for known nuclear states and chosen trigger intensities.
