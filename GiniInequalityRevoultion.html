Gini Differences Among Top 15 Countries by GDP (PPP)

Disclaimers & Notes:
1) The GDP values (in trillions of USD) are approximate 2023 Purchasing-Power-Parity (PPP) figures.
2) National Gini coefficients shown here are rough estimates from various public sources (World Bank, IMF, academic studies).
3) Subnational Gini data are not uniformly available, so the regional values are approximate best guesses, often inferred from partial stats or research. Real precision would require official household-level data for each region.
4) “Richest region” typically refers to a major subnational unit (state/province) with high GDP per capita; “poorest region” typically has the lowest per capita income. The Gini difference is the absolute difference between those two regional Ginis.
5) A difference > 0.10 in Gini suggests a large gap in inequality between the richest and poorest regions. Exact thresholds can vary depending on definitions and data sources.
6) All Gini coefficients are on a scale from 0 to 1 (0 = perfectly equal, 1 = perfectly unequal).
