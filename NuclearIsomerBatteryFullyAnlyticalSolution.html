Nuclear Isomer Battery: Full Analytical Solution

This document presents a closed-form analytical solution for the time evolution and power output of a theoretical nuclear isomer battery under triggered conditions. We assume:

  • You have a number N₀ of identical nuclear isomers, each in an excited state with energy E* above the ground state.
  • The isomer's half-life is T₁/₂, giving a spontaneous decay constant λ = ln(2)/T₁/₂.
  • At time t₀, a triggering mechanism is applied that induces an additional decay width Γᵗʳⁱᵍᵍᵉʳ, effectively increasing the decay rate.
  • Γ_spont = ℏ λ = ℏ ln(2)/T₁/₂, and Γ_trigger is determined by nuclear structure parameters and the external triggering field.

Population Evolution

Before triggering (t < t₀), the number of excited nuclei N(t) decays spontaneously:

N(t) = N₀ * exp(-λ t)

At t = t₀, we turn on the trigger, adding Γ_trigger. For t ≥ t₀:

N(t) = N₀ * exp(-λ t₀) * exp[-(λ + Γ_trigger/ℏ) (t - t₀)]

Combined into one piecewise definition:

For t < t₀: N(t) = N₀ * exp(-λ t) For t ≥ t₀: N(t) = N₀ * exp(-λ t₀) * exp[-(λ + Γ_trigger/ℏ)(t - t₀)]

Power Output

The instantaneous power output P(t) is the energy released per unit time. Each decay releases E*, and the total decay rate after triggering is (λ + Γ_trigger/ℏ). Since Γ_spont = ℏ λ, we can write:

P(t) = N(t) * E* * (Γ_spont + Γ_trigger)/ℏ

Before triggering (t < t₀):

P(t) = N₀ * E* * λ * exp(-λ t)

After triggering (t ≥ t₀), substitue N(t) from above and Γ_spont = ℏ λ:

P(t) = N₀ * E* * (Γ_spont + Γ_trigger)/ℏ * exp(-Γ_spont t₀/ℏ) * exp[-(Γ_spont + Γ_trigger)(t - t₀)/ℏ]

This gives a fully explicit closed-form solution for population N(t) and power P(t) given initial parameters and trigger conditions.

Triggering Width from Nuclear Structure

Γ_trigger depends on nuclear structure. For an E1 electromagnetic transition, a simplified approximate formula might be:

Γ_trigger ≈ (16π/9) * α * B(E1) * [ (E_γ)² * I_γ ] / [ℏ (m_p c²)]

Here:

  • α: fine-structure constant
  • B(E1): reduced transition probability for E1 transition
  • E_γ: photon energy
  • I_γ: photon intensity
  • m_p: proton mass (or chosen mass scale)

Substituting Γ_trigger back into the P(t) and N(t) formulas yields a fully analytic closed-form solution depending on known constants and experimental parameters.

Conclusion

We have derived a closed-form analytical formula for the population decay and power output of a nuclear isomer battery both in spontaneous and triggered regimes. By knowing E*, T₁/₂, B(E1), I_γ, E_γ, and other constants, one can directly compute N(t) and P(t) from the above equations, making this a closed-form analytic solution for the nuclear isomer battery scenario.
