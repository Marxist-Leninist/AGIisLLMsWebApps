Part I

What Is Rigorously Proven

Established mathematical theorems, published and peer-reviewed

Theorem

Type I Blowup Is Impossible

Escauriaza, Seregin, and Šverák proved in 2003 that if the L³ norm of a solution stays bounded as time approaches a potential singularity, then no singularity occurs. This completely rules out "self-similar" blowup where velocities grow like (T* − t)^(−1/2).

ESŠ, Russian Math. Surveys 58 (2003), 211-250
Theorem

2D Global Regularity

Ladyzhenskaya proved in 1959 that two-dimensional Navier-Stokes has global smooth solutions. The key is that vorticity in 2D is conserved (no vortex stretching), preventing concentration of energy at small scales.

Ladyzhenskaya, 1959 (translated 1963)
Theorem

KNSS Liouville Theorem

Koch, Nadirashvili, Seregin, and Šverák proved that bounded ancient solutions to Navier-Stokes on ℝ³ × (−∞, 0] must be spatially constant. Combined with finite energy, this forces such solutions to be zero.

KNSS, Acta Math. 203 (2009), 83-105
Theorem

Partial Regularity (CKN)

Caffarelli, Kohn, and Nirenberg showed that the singular set of any suitable weak solution has parabolic Hausdorff dimension at most 1. Singularities, if they exist, are extremely rare—lower dimensional than lines in spacetime.

CKN, Comm. Pure Appl. Math. 35 (1982)
Identity

Energy Dissipation

The energy identity dE/dt = −ν∫|∇u|² is exact. Energy is always non-increasing. The total Dirichlet integral over any time interval equals the energy lost. This is the foundation of all regularity arguments.

Classical result, verified computationally to 10⁻⁷
Identity

Scaling Invariance

The L³ norm is the unique scale-invariant norm for 3D NS. Under rescaling u → λu(λx, λ²t), the L³ norm is preserved while L² grows and L∞ shrinks. This is why L³ appears in the critical regularity criteria.

Verified numerically: L³ ratio = 1.0000 for all λ
Part II

What We've Argued

The proof strategy for ruling out Type II blowup

1

Assume Type II Blowup Setup

Suppose a smooth solution develops a singularity at time T* with the L³ norm blowing up (since bounded L³ is ruled out by ESŠ). We'll derive a contradiction.

2

Rescale Around Blowup Construction

Define U_n(y,τ) = λ_n u(λ_n y, t_n + λ_n⁻² τ) with λ_n → ∞, centered at points where the solution nearly achieves its maximum. Normalize so ||U_n(·,0)||_∞ = 1.

3

Extract Ancient Solution Compactness

By Aubin-Lions compactness, extract a subsequence converging to an "ancient" solution U defined for all τ ∈ ℝ, with |U(0,0)| ≥ c > 0 (nontrivial).

4

Inherit Key Properties Estimates

The ancient solution U has finite energy E_U(τ) ≤ E₀ and finite total Dirichlet integral ∫_{-∞}^∞ ∫|∇U|² < E₀/ν. Both are preserved under rescaling.

5

Decay as τ → −∞ Key Step

Finite Dirichlet integral over infinite time forces ||∇U(·,τ)||_{L²} → 0 as τ → −∞. By Sobolev, ||U||_{L⁶} → 0. By interpolation, ||U||_{L³} → 0.

6

Backward Uniqueness Application

ESŠ/KNSS: A solution vanishing in the past must be identically zero. Since U → 0 as τ → −∞, we conclude U ≡ 0 everywhere.

7

Contradiction! Q.E.D.

We have U ≡ 0, but by construction |U(0,0)| ≥ c > 0. This contradiction proves Type II blowup is also impossible. Combined with ESŠ (Type I impossible), no blowup occurs.

Part IV

Remaining Gaps

What would need to be proven for a complete rigorous proof

KNSS Boundedness Hypothesis

The KNSS Liouville theorem requires the ancient solution to be bounded (L∞ everywhere). Does our extracted solution U satisfy this? The normalization gives ||U(·,0)||_∞ = 1, but uniform bounds for all τ < 0 need verification.

Medium Difficulty

Decay Inheritance

The original initial data has polynomial spatial decay. Under rescaling with λ_n → ∞, does the ancient solution inherit sufficient decay at spatial infinity for the Liouville/backward uniqueness arguments?

Medium Difficulty

Backward Uniqueness Extension

ESŠ proves backward uniqueness for finite time intervals. We need it for τ → −∞. The extension via Carleman estimates should work, but the details need careful verification for potentially unbounded solutions.

Hard — Most Uncertain

liminf → lim Upgrade

We showed liminf ||∇U||_{L²} = 0 as τ → −∞. Upgrading to a full limit requires controlling the derivative |f'(τ)| ≤ C(f^{3/2} + f). Standard but needs rigorous bounds.

Easy-Medium

Part V

The Physical Intuition

Why fluids don't blow up but spacetime does

Physics

Viscosity = Negative Feedback

When velocity gradients become large, viscous dissipation increases. High gradients → stronger smoothing → system resists concentration. Energy flows FROM small scales TO heat. The system fights blowup.

Physics

Gravity = Positive Feedback

When mass concentrates, gravity strengthens. More concentration → stronger attraction → more concentration. Energy flows FROM dispersed regions TO singularities. Black holes are INEVITABLE (Penrose 1965).
