Warp Drive-Like Equation – Part 1

Part 1: Action and Positive-Energy Setup

This is the first part of our four-part exposition on constructing a stable, minimal-energy, positive-energy “warp drive–like” spacetime at subluminal speeds. Here, we present the baseline equations describing the field content and the action, setting the stage for the next sections.

1. Scalar Field Action

We introduce a real scalar field $\\phi(r)$ with a potential $V(\\phi)$. Gravity is described by the Einstein-Hilbert action. Let $G$ be Newton’s constant. In units where the speed of light $c=1$, the total action is:

$$ S = \\int d^4x \\sqrt{-g}\\, \\Bigl[ \\frac{R}{16\\pi G} \\;-\; \\tfrac{1}{2} g^{\\mu\\nu}\\partial_{\\mu}\\phi\\,\\partial_{\\nu}\\phi \\;-\; V(\\phi) \\Bigr]. $$

This setup ensures we stay within standard classical physics: no negative-energy terms appear, just a normal scalar field and the usual Einstein gravity. We will later show how to choose $V(\\phi)$ and $\\phi(r)$ to create our bubble-like configuration without invoking exotic matter.

2. Potential and Mass Scale

The potential $V(\\phi)$ is chosen to have a stable minimum at $\\phi_0$ with $V''(\\phi_0)=m^2\\;>\\;0$. This ensures the field near $\\phi_0$ behaves like a massive scalar, with mass $m$:

$$ m^2 = V''(\\phi_0) > 0. $$

Hence, around $\\phi_0$, no negative energy arises. The kinetic $(\\partial\\phi)^2$ and potential $(m^2 \\phi^2)$ terms remain strictly positive or at least non-negative.

3. No Negative Energy Required

Because the action contains only a standard scalar field, with a normal kinetic term and a well-chosen positive potential, there is no place for negative energy densities to appear.
